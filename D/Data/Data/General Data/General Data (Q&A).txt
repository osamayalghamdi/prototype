(Travel & Entry Requirements)
(Q) Do I need a visa to visit Saudi Arabia for a football match?
(A) Yes, most visitors need a visa. You can apply for a tourist eVisa online via https://visa.visitsaudi.com. Citizens of eligible countries can apply and receive approval within minutes.

(Q) Can I get a visa on arrival?
(A) Citizens from select countries (e.g., USA, UK, Schengen area) can get a visa on arrival.

(Q) Is a Hayya card required like during the 2022 Qatar World Cup?
(A) No, Saudi Arabia does not require a Hayya card. Only a valid match ticket and visa are typically needed.

(Q) What vaccinations or health checks are required?
(A) As of now, no mandatory vaccines are required, but COVID-19 vaccination or test results may be required based on airline or health regulations. Check the Saudi CDC or your airline for updates.

(Q) Can women travel alone to attend a football match?
(A) Yes, women can travel independently to Saudi Arabia and attend sporting events. Dress modestly and follow cultural guidelines.





(Arrival & Transport)
(Q) What is the best airport to fly into?
(A) Depends on the match location:
Riyadh: King Khalid International Airport (RUH)
Jeddah: King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED)
Dammam: King Fahd International Airport (DMM)

(Q) Is public transportation available to stadiums?
(A) Yes, in major cities like Riyadh and Jeddah, buses and ride-hailing apps (Uber, Careem) are reliable. Metro lines in Riyadh are expanding.

(Q) How do I travel between cities?
(A) Use domestic flights or the Haramain high-speed train (e.g., from Jeddah to Makkah or Medina).

(Q) Can I rent a car?
(A) Yes. A valid international driver’s license and passport are required. Drive on the right-hand side.

(Q) Is parking available at stadiums?
(A) Yes, but it can be crowded. Arrive early or use rideshares to avoid delays.





(Stadium & Match Info)
(Q) What are the most famous football stadiums in Saudi Arabia?
(A) King Fahd International Stadium (Riyadh)
King Abdullah Sports City (Jeddah)
Prince Mohammed bin Salman Stadium (Al Hilal home)
Mrsool Park (Riyadh)

(Q) Where can I buy tickets?
(A) Through https://ticketmx.com or club websites. Tickets for major events may sell out quickly.

(Q) Are there separate sections for families or women?
(A) No. As of 2018, all fans can sit together, though family sections are still available.

(Q) Can I bring food and drink into the stadium?
(A) No. Outside food and drink are not allowed. Concessions are available inside.

(Q) Is alcohol allowed in stadiums?
(A) No, alcohol is strictly prohibited in Saudi Arabia in all public and private settings.

(Q) Are matches shown in English or only Arabic?
(A) Announcements are mostly in Arabic, but English services are increasing at international matches.

(Q) Are there VIP or hospitality packages?
(A) Yes. Many stadiums offer VIP seating, private boxes, and lounge access for premium ticket holders.

(Accommodation & Food)
(Q) Are hotels near stadiums available?
(A) Yes. International chains (Hilton, Marriott) and local hotels are available in Riyadh, Jeddah, and Dammam.

(Q) Is Airbnb allowed in Saudi Arabia?
(A) Yes. Airbnb and similar platforms operate legally in the country.

(Q) Are restaurants open late after matches?
(A) Many eateries are open until midnight or later, especially in big cities.

(Q) Is food expensive in stadiums?
(A) Not usually. Expect moderate prices for snacks and fast food.

(Q) Can I find vegetarian or vegan food?
(A) Yes. Many restaurants offer vegetarian options. Vegan options are more limited but growing.





(Technology & Connectivity)
(Q) Can I use my mobile phone and internet?
(A) Yes. Buy a local SIM card (e.g., STC, Zain, Mobily) at the airport.

(Q) Is public Wi-Fi available in stadiums?
(A) Not always. It’s best to use mobile data.

(Q) Which apps should I download before coming?
(A) Careem/Uber (transport)
Google Maps
STC Pay or Apple Pay
TicketMX (for tickets)





(Culture & Etiquette)
(Q) Is it safe to visit Saudi Arabia for sports?
(A) Yes. Saudi Arabia is generally safe for tourists, especially in major cities.

(Q) Is it okay to cheer loudly during matches?
(A) Yes. Fans are passionate and vocal. Just avoid offensive gestures or chants.

(Q) Can I wear my team jersey?
(A) Yes. Wear club or national team jerseys. Avoid anything offensive or political.

(Q) What should women wear?
(A) Modest clothing (e.g., long sleeves, loose pants).

(Q) Are there rules on taking photos in stadiums?
(A) You can take photos of the game, but avoid photographing people without permission.

(Q) Can I bring a flag or banner?
(A) Yes, if it’s not offensive. Check stadium rules for dimensions.





(Money & Costs)
(Q) What is the currency in Saudi Arabia?
(A) Saudi Riyal (SAR)

(Q) Are credit cards accepted?
(A) Yes, widely. Visa, MasterCard, Apple Pay, and Mada are common.

(Q) How much is a football match ticket?
(A) Prices vary:
Regular: 30–100 SAR (~$8–27)
VIP: 300–1,000+ SAR

(Q) Are stadium souvenirs expensive?
(A) Jerseys and merchandise are reasonably priced compared to European markets.





(Safety & Legal)
(Q) Are there any laws I should be aware of?
(A) No alcohol or drugs 
Dress modestly
Avoid public displays of affection
Respect prayer times and cultural norms

(Q) Is crowd control well managed?
(A) Yes. Police and event staff are present. Follow signs and instructions.

(Q) Is insurance required?
(A) Travel insurance is recommended, and some visas require proof of it.

(Q) What do I do if I lose my passport or need help?
(A) Visit your country’s embassy or consulate in Riyadh or Jeddah.





(Before You Go)
(Q) What weather should I expect?
(A)Summer: Very hot (up to 45°C / 113°F)
Winter: Mild and pleasant (20–30°C / 68–86°F)

(Q) Do I need to speak Arabic?
(A) No, but learning basics helps. English is widely understood in cities and at events.

(Q) Is it worth attending a match in Saudi Arabia?
(A) Absolutely. The Saudi Pro League is rapidly growing, with world-class players and passionate fans in state-of-the-art stadiums.
